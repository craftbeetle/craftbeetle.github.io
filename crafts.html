CRAFTS

If I made it, painted it, crafted it, and it's more artistic than practical then it will be here. I don't consider myself an artist, but more of a craftsman, hence the name. Still, I do occasionally make decorative things.

Princess

A commission painting for a friend. It was a really fun painting process because I got to do a nice gradient and speckle some stars. I don't think I would have painted a princess miniature if it wasn't for someone else.

Vecna

I painted this resin printed Vecna mini as a commission for a friend. It was a relatively straight forward painting process, thought I had to be careful not to break the fragile ribbons and to match the color of the original Vecna art.

3D Scanned Man and Scanner

A client gave me a 3D scan of the man and of the 3D scanner he is wearing. I then cleaned up the models in Blender, almost completely remodeling the scanner, and printed them on my resin printer. I then painted them. They were very happy with the result and I got to try out new techniques and flex my modeling skills. The skin was definitely the hardest part.

Deathwatch Watch Master

I first got into Warhammer 40K to play Killteam with one of my best friends. I figured what better faction than the Deathwatch, the originators of the Killteams. They looked super cool and were very customizable so I picked up a box and got to it. What I had failed to understand was that I did not have an infinite budget, nor did I have infinite time to paint, learn, and play. I now have a lot of cool minis that I do nothing with. This guy turned out pretty good.

Rucksack

I designed and made this rucksack in my junior year of highschool and it has served me well for many years. I used it daily through the end of high school, all of college, and for years at work, some 7 years in total. I recently purchased a Goruck GR1 for travel and work and I am very happy with it, but this rucksack is still my go-to for outings to the beach or event near home. I made it with real canvas and Palestinian suede. The inner lining is cotton. I hand stitched it all while binge watching Narcos.

Borderlands Psycho Mask

I really enjoyed Borderlands 2 and so when the time came to decide what I wanted my first ever cosplay to be, the Borderlands psycho won out. The outfit consisted of pants I dyed orange and painted, boots I borrowed from a neighbor, various knee/shoulder/elbow pads that I added spikes to, and this hand made mask, all covered in hand drawn cell-shading lines, including my bare torso. I made the mask out of air dry clay over a paper and glue base, the lenses are from cheap plastic sunglasses, the respirator is a computer fan, and I added leather straps to the front and to make it wearable.

I had a great time at the convention, spent quality time with my friends, and ended up winning an MTD draft, pulling a very rare card and selling it, which made me back my entry ticket cost.
