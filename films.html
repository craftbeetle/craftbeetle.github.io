FILMS

My thoughts on the films I have watched. This list only goes back a few years but in that time I have watched a lot of movies. I don't have a lot to say about every movie, sometimes it's just something I watched a few years back.

2025

The Eid Gift

The Zionists steal your home, murder your family, ethnically cleanse the land, wage genocide on your people, and occasionally offer you the opportunity to visit home (you must pay them for the privilege of course and purchase their goods while you are there.) do you take it offer? It might be the only time you ever get to see your stolen home. You might be martyred by them before the next chance, before liberation. This impossible question is at the center of the documentary short film The Eid Gift. The answer may appear clear now but the reality is far more difficult.

Salt of this Sea

It's one thing to read about Palestine, talk to Palestinians, watch the news of Palestinian events, it's a whole other thing to experience Palestinian stories. The film may be fictional, but the checkpoints, the harassment, the abuse, the danger, the dispossession, the frustration and anger and pain. That is all real.

Salt of this Sea follows a Palestinian women as she returns to her homeland and finds it filled with a hostile and brutal occupier. Her mobility, language, appearance, love, and mere existence is an act of rebellion against the tyrannical apartheid state. You see plainly the Banality of Evil as everyone from TSA to illegal occupier participates in the oppression. Even those who seem outwardly progressive are shown to be simply acting the part in the shallowest sense.

This is a surprisingly bad movie, it's not even funny bad. Devoid of context and stakes, poorly set up and completely shallow and unrelatable characters. These aren't even high standards in children's cartoons, there are other Pokemon movies and shows with far higher quality. Anyway, this is junk.

Nothing short of amazing. The film is slow rolling crime and the noose gradually tightens until the end where some semblance of justice is served. Then the film goes "Oh by the way, all of that happened for real" and you can't help but feel it all over again. Worth every minute.

Barbie

Capitalism is not the solution to oppression, it is the root cause. Women becoming the oppressor solves nothing and isn't what feminists at large want. But the film clearly would rather present a "Conservative Capitalist" antagonist who is defeated at then end of the film not through revolution but through willingly transforming into "Progressive Capitalism". The film claims that nothing really needs to change in the world, we just need some female representation in the oppressive class. But no. Capitalism can cover itself in Kente cloth, wear high heels, hold a rainbow flag, but it is still the cause of the world suffering. Don't be fooled by false promises.

Bad pacing, bad visuals, boring acting, didn't like HAL. Maybe I'm just a hater (entirely possible) or maybe the book set too high of a bar (unlikely, the book wasn't mind blowing either) but I just hate this movie.

2021

Dune - Part One

Back when the film was first announced I immediately knew I had to read the book first. So I made sure not to watch any trailers (as usual) and read the book and loved it. Then I watched the film on release. There are whole massive chunks of the book entirely missing, but what is shown is incredible. I was wholly engrossed for the entirety of the film. The sound, the music, the visuals, the whole experience was exceptional. One nitpick is that the arabic pronunciations are abysmal, were there no available language coaches? Anyway, I eagerly await the second film.
