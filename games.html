GAMES

Here live some of my thoughts on the games I have completed or played enough of. Sometimes I have a lot to say about the games I spend hours playing. Sometimes I play a game for a few hours, think it's neat, and put it down forever.

2025

Ape Out

Phenomenal sound design paired with a single visceral gameplay mechanic accompanied by very simple visuals that fit perfectly. It was a joy to play Ape Out. The game is clearly made with a lot of love and care and immense creativity.

Aperture Desk Job

Funny silly toilet desk game.

Metal Gear Rising: Revengence

I admit that before playing Metal Gear Rising: Revengence, I was not a fan of Kojima's. Maybe even a hater. Not in the true sense, I really loved the lore and world he made, but I could only ever enjoy it through lore videos and never could get into the games. I think that was partially because the first game of his I tried to play was MGS V which has a 2 hour intro that is mostly cutscenes and by the time I finished it I was already burned out and put the game down. Not to mention that when I first tried MGR I played it on Difficult, died once, and gave up. All that to say I came in with the wrong attitude and expectations. Years passed and I decided to give MGR another chance, if only to see Senator Armstrong. I was patient with the controls, respected the difficulty, sat back and enjoyed the cut scenes and low and behold it all clicked.

I love this game, it's silly, it's 2010s awesome, the music bangs, the action is over the top, the ideology isn't bad, and the pacing is perfect. When I played engaged with the game how it wanted to be engaged with I realized that the point of the cut scenes was to give you a bit of relief from the action. With this new perspective I am considering playing the other games in the series in release order to actually appreciate the overarching story being told. It really did help that I played it on my Steam Deck instead of my PC, being able to lay comfortably in bed makes the long cutscenes far more palatable.

PS: I love Jetstream Sam and Senator Armstrong. Blade Wolf is the goodest boy.

OlliOlli World

I got OlliOlli World because I enjoyed my time with OlliOlli2 - Welcome to Olliwood. World has a good character customization system and an actual plot and characters, but neither of those things is the point of the game and they have no real effect on it. I was disappointed with the actual gameplay. It's relatively shallow, repetitive, and mostly grinds. There is little feedback on actions taken and basically no need to do complex moves, other than the score. Famous 3D skating games succeed when they provide many challenges of different types: Speed, style, combo length, jumps, specific moves in order, etc...

I completed one of four worlds and did not feel compelled to continue playing.

2024

Need For Speed III: Hot Pursuit

I am a huge fan of 5th generation graphics. I didn't play video games back then so I don't have nostalgia for the style, I just think it looks really nice. In the same way that photographs didn't make the painting obsolete, realistic 3D graphics do not make crunchy low poly graphics obsolete. That being said I don't love everything about that era of games: the low FPS, the pop-in, and specifically the PS1's warble.

Retro 3D game preamble completed, Need for Speed III: Hot Pursuit is really fun! Looks amazing, sounds real good, hard to drive but once it clicks it clicks. At first I was bumping off the walls and crashing horribly in the hazards, but after some practice I was sticking to the line and drifting just the right amount. Highly enjoyable!

BattleBit Remastered

Impressive how much a three man team can achieve when concerned only with good gameplay and not worrying about the best graphics or the most aggressive monetization or advertising. It's especially poignant as BattleBit Remastered released around the same time the newest Battlefield game flopped. This, and the success of many other indie games, as well as the slew of recent AAA flops and layoffs is a clear sign of the readjustment of the industry. Interest free money is gone, the period of massive growth is over, labor is up, and the video game corporations are being out competed by solo devs and indie studios.

That all being said, and as much fun as I had with this game, at time of writing it seems to have been abandoned by the developers which is actually a bit of a rare case when it comes to success indie games. AAA corporation on the other hand are more than happy to shutter whole studios making widely popular games simply because profits weren't high enough.

Sifu

I had a passing interest in the game and finally decided to pick it up after Jacob Geller made a video about it. It has such an amazing progression, from the episodic aging to the true ending. I started out as I assume most do, running around getting hit, dying, barely making it through levels. But as I got better at the game I slowly mastered the flow of bait and punish, as well as the rush and strike. I easily made it through large portions of the game untouched and finally finished the game first in the bad ending and then the good. The bonus stages were also a blast. In a strange turn of events, this game caused me to want to play Sekiro. I needed more of that beautiful parrying gameplay.
Side note: I played on Linux and experienced a GPU memory leak. The game would get progressively slower until it became a slide show and I had to reboot it. I finally found the cause in one of the graphics settings and it was fixed.

2023

Cyberpunk 2077

I have to admit I was a huge hater when this game came out. I had absolutely no hype for it, didn't watch any trailers and new very little about it. So to me, all I saw was a game struggling and floundering, and developer promises broken and a poor product sold at full price. I wrote the game off and moved on. Fast forward to the 2.1 patch. On a whim I broke my promise of never playing this game and bought it and the DLC on sale. Little did I know the game would become one of my favorites of all time. I think it really helped that I didn't have any expectation for the game, I know a lot of people expected an RPG with dialogue and decisions and character progression, and of course were disappointed with a mostly linear game. Meanwhile I came in expecting to shoot a bunch of goons for about 10 hours and was happily surprised with 100 hrs of beautiful realistic and well acted characters, a compelling story, and very fun gameplay. Johnny Silverhand lives rent free in my head.

2021

ABZÛ

Beautiful and accurate sea life. I enjoy a good aquarium and I've been to one of the world's largest as can be seen in my critters page. But it always feels wrong to observe an animal in captivity, clearly they are not meant to be where we have put them, and so they will never behave how they truly would in their natural environment. ABZÛ attempted, and I would argue succeeded, in letting you unobtrusively observe aquatic environments up close and personal. It will never be at good as deep sea diving, but I don't intend on ever doing that so this is more than good enough. Highly recommend to all.

Age of Empires II

Age of Empires II has a special place in my heart. I haven't played it in a long time and I was never particularly good at it but it is one of the oldest games I remember playing, alongside minesweeper and pinball. I absolutely love the art, the way the units move, the carefully designed buildings. The sound effects are ingrained into my brain. I'm no good at RTS games, I don't have the will to get good, but AoE2 will always be one of my favorite games of all time.
