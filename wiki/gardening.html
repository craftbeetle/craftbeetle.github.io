GARDENING

I have loved gardening ever since I learned how to sprout beans on the kitchen windowsill. I am still learning. I've had good success with beans and tomatoes.

One of the keys to successful and sustainable gardening is effective water management.

Food sovereignty

The great majority of people cannot be self sufficient in food production, those are spend the majority of their time doing so. An individualistic survival garden is not the goal, instead my aim with gardening is to produce a significant quantity of food such that it reduces reliance on commercial agriculture. I am unlikely to ever grow grains such as rice or wheat, nor do I ever expect to make my own olive oil or salt. What far more feasible is producing fruits, herbs, vegetables, and protein with the use of animals such as chickens, ducks, rabbits, and fish.

Composting

One positive consequence of introducing gardening to our lives is diverting a waste product from landfill and instead using it as input for our food production. Peels, ends, coffee grounds, used tea, egg shells, leaves, cardboard, and plenty of other organic matter is composed (relatively) effortlessly into food for our garden.

Even if we composed then simply spread the compost on the lawn (don't do that, kill your lawn), it would still be far better for our environment that having a garbage truck take it to a landfill where it will leak methane into the atmosphere.

Economics of Gardening

Inputs Optimization
Sun Garden placement, planting locations based on plant height and sun needs.
Water Water Management - Using less water, collecting rain, keeping water in the soil.
Nutrients Using the right nutrients at the right time, composting waste.
Labor Automating irrigation, solarizing and mulching to destroy weeds, good time management and planning.
Seeds/Bulbs Using heirloom seeds, reusing seeds from previous season.


Outputs Uses
Produce Edible products from gardening.
Feed Feed for animals.
Organic Material Material that will be composted or otherwise returned to the soil. Can also be used as fill for raised garden beds.
Construction/Crafting Material Wood, fibers, and other products that can be used to make things.
Fuel Cooking, pottery firing, gasification, heating.
Personal Benefit The intangible benefits of going outside, touching some dirt, watching your effort turn into life, sharing with others.
