Imperial Super Profits

26.04.12 BACK TO BLOGS

If material conditions shape one's perception of reality and their class consciousness then what is the effect of imperial super profits on a population? What happens to a society supplemented by wealth extracted from the colonies and distributed through over-inflated wages and cheap consumer goods and energy?

A normal society benefits from the value of its own labor. Whether that value is evenly distributed is another question, but the value is a sum total of a the people's effort. In the empire, the value being distributed is a sum of the imperial citizen's labor and the colonial subject's labor. Empire is a violent and painful endeavor and thus the imperial citizens, those closest in physical proximity to the seat of power and tasked with maintaining and extending that power, are bribed to continue empire. This bribe is why imperial citizens do not revolt against the empire, why those in the belly of the beast, no matter how much they think they are suffering, can never muster a resistance. They have so many treats to lose. Even the potential of treats keeps them in line.

These treats, the imperial super profits, have a distorting effect on reality. Let us take cheap energy as an example. While the rest of the world must naturally be efficient in their use of energy and design their cities, homes, jobs, and lives to accommodate it, the imperial citizen behaves incoherently. The imperial citizen cools their home in the summer with AC then puts on a sweater and sleeps under a blanket. They heat their homes in the winter then wear shorts and eat ice cream. They build towers of glass with no regard for sun direction. They drive massive gas guzzling trucks just to seem cool. This is the behavior of a spoiled child. One that lives in unreality.

Or let us take hygiene. All over the world people know that they must wash their hands frequently, bath regularly, wear masks, immunize, wash their groceries, sanitize surfaces, and generally take all necessary precautions to prevent disease. Prevention is much cheaper than the cure. And we see this reflected in their healthcare and public health messaging and policy as well. Yet in the empire we do not see the same behavior. The imperial citizen is a gross animal because they can afford to be. The can choose not to wash their hands, not to bath, not to eat well and try to prevent sickness because over the counter medicine is cheap and abundant and even the healthcare, as expensive and unaffordable as it is, is still far more accessible than in the majority of the world. The imperial citizen is more concerned about keeling the cash flowing than they are about killing mass swathes of their own population through the spread of preventable disease.

Rent-A-Friend

These inflated wages have more sinister effects than simply wasting massive amounts of energy, and polluting excessively, and allowing the imperial citizen to be gross and lethally inconsiderate, they tear apart the fabric of society itself. In the rest of the world wages are low because they the majority if the value is being extracted by the empire. This causes the citizens of the world to need to work together and get along to survive. The imperial citizen on the other hand replaces human interactions with transactions. Instead of making relationships with people they buy prostitutes and online models and porn. Instead of living communally they each have a little castle that none may encroach upon at pain of death. Instead of conflict resolution and restorative justice they resort to legalism and police.

This atomized imperial citizen is cut off from the world. All friction is lubricated away with money. All interactions are voluntary and pleasurable. Thus their tolerance for difference and disruption diminishing. Their ability to weather difficult times is reduced because they have no networks of support. When the cash flows they coerce others to do their bidding, when the cash dries up they live on the street.

The imperial citizen is an abnormality in their private life and an atomized weakling in society, but the problems don't stop there. On the international level the imperial citizen is woefully left behind.

House Cats

Those who live in the real world must know how it works to survive it. Citizens of the world literally cannot afford to ignore politics and global affairs. They must be fully knowledgeable of as must of the world as possible if they are to have a chance to make it. Like a rabbit in the forest, their eyes and ears are ever observing and learning.

The imperial citizen, cushioned from reality by wealth, and afford to ignore it altogether. Their government bullies the world into submission, their fat wallets absorb the blows of economic instability. Wheat prices increasing by a dollar per pound will cripple vast swathes of the planet and cause untold famines, but in the imperial core is will barely be noticed. The only price imperial citizens are even aware of is gas, and even that would have to double or triples before it really shakes them.

Being protected from reality makes one soft and maladapted. The people of the world are mutts, dogs and cats of no pedigree, living in dangerous and unforgiving environments, prepared to take a beating and survive it, sharp instincts and fast reflexes. The imperial citizen is as a pug or hairless and declawed cat, barely able to breath, unable to defend itself or even be in the real world for more than a few moment at a time, living indoors with the support of their owners.

It is an imperial privilege to not know. There are no children in Palestine who do not know the sight of death and the weight of rubble. There are no children in Lebanon who do not know the sounds of a Reaper drone. There are no girls in Iran who do not know that Americans blow up schools, killing 175 little girls and the parents who came to save them, then double tapping them to make sure. There are no black boys in Amerika who do not know that death stalks them wearing a badge. There are no Latin American children who do not know that kidnapping and trafficking is state policy. And yet, speak to an imperial citizen of any age and notice that they know nothing. They their children "must be protected from knowing" and that their adults "just don't do politics".

This is all fine and good until it isn't.

Over time these maladapted imperial citizens filter deeper and deeper into the machinery of empire and start to run the show. At first you can hardly notice, the momentum of empire carries it forward and enough competent people remain to keep the system ticking over. But it only gets worse over time. With no natural selection to filter the morons out, they only get more entrenched. They get over confident, meanwhile their colonized victims have remained studious.

Alley Cats

If material conditions shape one's perception of reality and their class consciousness then what is the effect of total deprivation and siege on a population? What happens to a society strangled by empire and threated with destruction?

It is only natural then that Operation al Aqsa Flood, the solidaritous support of Hezbollah and Ansar Allah, and the Ramadan War would be the foil to the empire's hegemony.

The empire grows blind with power and fat on stolen wealth while the colonized sharpen their knives, eyes piercing, minds ablaze, stomachs growling.
