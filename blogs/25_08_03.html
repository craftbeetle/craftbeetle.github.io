First Year Review

25.08.03 BACK TO BLOGS

Last year I decided to finally take the plunge and make a personal website. It wasn't my first attempt but it was my first real attempt. I had made a few websites before but they were empty, directionless, and devoid of soul. This time I wanted to do it right so I watched some tutorials, read some articles, browsed Neocities and then got to writing HTML and CSS.

One year later, here we are. Hundreds of nature photos, dozens of books, shows, films, and games reviewed. Many blog and micro-blogs written. A myriad of projects, artworks, game dev projects documented. And a growing page of links you should check out after you're done reading.

Looking back at the first year of Craft Beetle I feel proud of what I made and more importantly proud of how regularly I updated it and continue to do so. So often I start something great and never finish it, or do one and drop the series. This website is a testament to consistency above all else, consistent updates, consistent theme, consistent ideals.

I created Craft Beetle as a rebellion against the commercial web, advertizement, capitalism, imperialism, consumerism, and the associated ills. I wanted this site to be a home for my thoughts and creations, not all of them, but those meant to be shared with the world. I know some of my friends regularly visit my site and that means the world to me. I intentionally did not add any tracking or visiting stats to Craft Beetle, both because I deeply care about privacy, but also because I knew that if I had a number telling me how many people visited my site I would become obsessed. So instead I ask you, the reader, to send me an email. I think it would be really cool and deeply human, don't be shy, I'm a regular human on the other end of the screen too.

Looking forward, I'm hoping for more consistency as well as some new pages, tweaks, and upgrades moving forward. I think I built a really solid foundation and I now feel comfortable trying to get more creative. I hope to see you soon for another update.
