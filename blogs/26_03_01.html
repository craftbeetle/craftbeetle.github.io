Apothecary, Utopian and Scientific

26.03.01 BACK TO BLOGS

Islam and Science

I grew up in a Muslim and scientific family, which includes my extended family on both sides. I was raised around doctors, physicists, accountants, engineers, farmers, nurses, bankers, you name it. Many of my family members are teachers from kindergarten to university in science, math, medicine, Islam, history, Arabic, and more. I say this to as context for my upbringing as someone who believes strongly in Islam and science without any contradiction.

In my understanding, Allah created everything and it's our duty to try to figure out how it all works. In fact, it is a form of worship to do rigorous science in pursuit of understanding the mechanisms of the world. There are things we will never understand or have evidence for: the nature of the soul, angels and devils, evil magic, miracles. But, that does not preclude us from studying what Allah has made understandable: physics, medicine, history, law, economy, psychology, art.

This understanding has caused me to be naturally anti-superstitious. If it's not Allah, and it's not science, then it cannot exist. I still strongly believe this.

Health and Capitalism

If you live in Amerika or a similar western country you have surely noticed that the health care system is designed to squeeze maximum profit from the most desperate. It is a really good business model to monopolize the means of staying alive and healthy and selling it at extreme prices to people who have no other choice. It isn't the fault of any individual doctor or nurse or pharmacist, though some are actively complicit, but instead it is the capitalism system natural consequences.

This system has various effects. Some access modern medicine by paying the costs, or through insurance, or go bankrupt trying to stay alive. Others visit more civilized countries such as Cuba and Mexico to have operations and purchase cheaper medicine. And other still forgo modern medicine all together, with various reasons given: "Men don't go to the doctor", or "I cannot afford insulin AND rent", or "They're going to microchip me with the vaccine", etc...

The cumulative effects of this capitalist mode of health care on society is widespread distrust, disuse, and hostility towards modern medicine. This is of course compounded upon by genuinely evil experiments and torture conducted by the Amerikan government on blacks (Tuskegee Syphilis Study, Amerikan Gynecology Experiments on Enslaved African Women, Black Pain Tolerance Myth, Henrietta Lacks, etc), on Puerto Ricans (Forced Sterilizations, Testing of Illicit Drugs, etc), and on numerous other peoples including the Opioid Epidemic, MK-ULTRA, Vipeholm Experiments, Gasoline Baths, and many more.

This all to say that I empathize with and understand the many who want nothing to do with modern medicine. I have experienced modern medicine as practiced outside of Amerika, by kind doctors from the people, and by close family members, but those who only have a Western experience with it are excused in (wrongly) believing it to be all bad.

Islam and Medicine

Due to the aforementioned love of science in the Islamic world, it is no surprise that much of modern medicine was invented by Muslim scholars. The Canon of Medicine was the masterwork of Ibn Sina (Avicenna), among his many other colossal contributions to many fields of science, philosophy, astronomy, and poetry.

The eye's optical properties, cataracts removal surgery, free hospitals for all, mental health facilities, an abundance of elite female doctors, the great works of Al Razi and Al Kindi and Ibn Al Nafis, are all just a few of the countless ways that the Muslim world excelled in and pioneered medicine.

Apothecary vs Snake Oil

We have now established my priors, the history of medical malpractice in the Amerikan Empire, and the Islamic contributions to medicine. That was all in an effort to explain my fundamental unease and distrust of what is commonly called "Natural Medicine".

My automatic reaction to natural medicine is to dismiss it as snake oil and affirm the correctness of modern medicine. That has been the way I have dealt with claims that this or that cause or cure cancer, that this herb or oil or whatnot has alleviate this symptom or the other, that medicine is all fake and the solutions are all "natural". Good luck "naturally" setting a broken bone or removing a burst appendix.

But as with all things, more life experience and knowledge bring about changes in beliefs if one is sincere and open. While my instinct is to refuse all natural medicines and stick to the modern medicine, I cannot help but acknowledge that doctors have been paid to prescribe Opioids. That capitalism derives more profits from modern medicine if all alternatives, viable or not, are discarded out and hand and derided. That naturally existing substances have physical and psychological effects: caffeine in coffee and tea, opium from poppies, psilocybin in mushrooms, alcohol from fermented sugars, and so on. That the Quran and Prophet peace and blessings be upon him praise certain foods like dates, honey, black seed, milk, olive oil, vinegar, figs, among others.

Reevaluating Health

I have thus been forced to reevaluate a subject I had once thought so simple. Clearly, modern medicine is very effective at dealing with acute ailments and injuries and immunize against diseases, and applied correctly it can be highly effective as in the Cuban example, but due to it's close relationship with capitalism in the west it has alienated vast swathes of the people and failed to prevent disease. It puts profits above all which inevitably results in poorer health outcomes, denied care, misdiagnosis and prescription, and a bevy of ethical transgressions.

On the other hand, natural medicine's loudest champions are grifters preying on desperate people. Snake oil salesmen, unregulated "supplements", dubious health claims, false advertisement, fake super foods, "detoxification" "anti-oxidants" and "inflammation reduction" (do they think we are rusty dumpster fires? What toxins? What oxidation? What inflammation?), and a scorn for science makes sifting truth from falsehood an onerous task.

And yet, as I have shown, there is certainly merit in both. I have yet more to learn but it is now clear that foods and other natural substances have undeniable effects that can be harnessed to prevent disease and improve daily life. At the same time, trusted doctors and other medical professionals are indispensable in diagnosing and treating many diseases and injuries.

My task now is to systematically study apothecary, discern truth from fiction, and cautiously implement what I have learned into my diet. At the very least it will get me to drink more varied teas and try more exotic seasonings, but it may also have more potent effects such as better sleep, easier digestions, and maybe more.
