Bumper Crop Standard

26.04.25 BACK TO BLOGS

Westoid quality of life standards are based on a temporary imperial surplus generated from the second impartial war. It is as if a farmer gets a bumper crop and plans for it never to run out.

In the west it is expected that one moves out at 18, lives independently on their own wages, marries and buys a "starter home" at 21, then goes on to build wealth and buy a larger home and so on and so forth. Included in the delusion is multiple international vacations, many cars, and a sizable retirement. This is all built on the abnormal economic bump that the west enjoyed after the destruction of the planet in the second imperial war, aka WW2.

Farmers get about the same harvest year to year, with occasional major variations due to extra rain or drought. A harvest that significantly exceeds expectations is called a bumper crop. It has little to do with the farmers actions and much more to do with climate happenstance. Only a foolish farmer would start spending more, expecting every year to be a number crop.

The west has experienced a bumper half century and has convinced itself that it not only earned it through hard work, but also that it will never end. That, without putting more effort, life in the west will remain at the artificially high standards set by the post war prosperity. But that obviously was not going to be the case, and the game was up as early at the 80s if not earlier.

Material reality collides with imperial core fantasy but the impact is felt in slow motion. Like a slap slowly propagating across their face. It is very common to hear complains about how the west use to be great, how one (White) man could support a whole family on his labor alone (and the unpaid domestic labor of his wife, and the vastly underpaid labor of black and brown men and women). How homes use to be cheap and life use to be easy. That was all bumper crop. The rest of the world has never lived like that, and it is foolish to expect it last.

Perhaps had the west not been a capitalist empire, the bumper decades could have been invested into long term benefit. Like a farmer using their bumper revenue to invest in better irrigation instead of spending it on steak.

Now we reap what we sow. Nothing we do now will keep the age old unmaintained infrastructure from crumbling, the power grid from failing, the neutered education system from producing human duds. The west has set itself up to fail, expecting every year to be a bumper year. The rest of the world tightened their belt, took millions of death on the chin, and forged ahead to build their futures while we squandered our and sabotaged theirs.
